Meeting Title: Acme Corp Merger Strategy Discussion
Participants:
- Michael Grant (CEO, Acme Corp)
- Laura Chen (General Counsel, Acme Corp)
- David Rosen (External Legal Partner)
- Priya Shah (Associate)
- James Liu (Investment Advisor)

---

Michael: Thanks everyone for joining on short notice. As you know, we are moving forward with the potential merger with Beta Industries, and we need to align on legal strategy before we proceed to the next stage.

Laura: Yes, from an internal perspective, we’ve completed the initial commercial assessment. The key issue now is structuring the deal in a way that minimizes regulatory friction, especially given Beta’s exposure in multiple jurisdictions.

David: Understood. From our side, the first thing we need to decide is whether we’re pursuing a stock purchase or an asset purchase. Each has different implications from a liability and tax standpoint.

James: From a financial perspective, a stock purchase is cleaner and faster, but it does mean Acme would inherit all existing liabilities, including any unknown risks.

Priya: We’ve done a preliminary review of Beta’s disclosures, but we haven’t completed full due diligence yet. There are some flagged items in their environmental compliance history that we need to investigate further.

Michael: That’s exactly my concern. I don’t want us inheriting hidden liabilities that could become an issue post-merger.

Laura: In that case, we might want to consider structuring this as a hybrid deal, or at least ensure strong indemnity protections in the agreement.

David: Agreed. We can draft indemnification clauses that protect Acme against pre-closing liabilities, but enforcement depends on Beta’s financial strength post-transaction.

James: Another point to consider is regulatory approval. Given the size of both companies, this transaction will likely trigger antitrust review in at least the US and EU.

Priya: Yes, and depending on the jurisdictions involved, we may need to file pre-merger notifications. That could impact our timeline significantly.

Michael: What kind of timeline are we realistically looking at?

David: Best case, if there are no major regulatory concerns, we’re looking at 8 to 12 weeks for approvals. Worst case, it could extend to several months if there are challenges.

Laura: We should build that uncertainty into our planning. Also, we need to think about employee transition and retention. That’s not strictly legal, but it will impact how we draft certain provisions.

Priya: On the due diligence side, we’ll need full access to Beta’s contracts, IP portfolio, litigation history, and compliance records. I can coordinate the data room setup.

David: Yes, Priya, please take the lead on that. We need a comprehensive diligence checklist by tomorrow so we can send it to Beta’s legal team.

Priya: Understood, I’ll prepare and circulate the checklist by end of day tomorrow.

Michael: Good. What about confidentiality? We need to ensure this doesn’t leak before we’re ready.

Laura: We already have an NDA in place, but I’ll review it again to ensure it covers all aspects of this transaction, including advisors and third parties.

David: It might be worth updating the NDA to include stricter provisions around information sharing, especially given the sensitivity of this deal.

James: Agreed. Market perception will be critical here.

Michael: Let’s make sure that’s handled immediately.

Laura: I’ll take that action and revert with an updated draft.

David: Another important piece is the term sheet. We should aim to have a non-binding term sheet ready before entering detailed negotiations.

Michael: How soon can we get that done?

David: If we align on key commercial terms this week, we can have a draft ready early next week.

James: I can work with Michael to finalize the financial terms so legal can incorporate them into the draft.

Michael: Yes, let’s do that. James, can you send me a revised valuation model by tomorrow?

James: Yes, I’ll send that by tomorrow afternoon.

Priya: For the term sheet, I’ll start preparing a template so we can plug in the agreed terms quickly.

David: Good. Also, we need to think about governance post-merger. Board composition, voting rights, and management structure should be addressed early.

Laura: That’s a good point. We should also consider any change-of-control clauses in Beta’s existing contracts.

Priya: I’ll include that in the diligence checklist.

Michael: Excellent. What about communication with Beta? Who’s taking the lead there?

David: I can coordinate directly with their external counsel, but we should have a single point of contact internally.

Laura: I’ll take that role from Acme’s side.

Michael: Perfect.

David: To summarize next steps:
- Priya will prepare the diligence checklist
- Laura will review and update the NDA
- James will finalize valuation inputs
- We will aim to draft a term sheet early next week

Michael: That sounds right.

Laura: One more thing — we should schedule a follow-up meeting early next week to review progress and address any issues from the diligence process.

Priya: I can coordinate calendars and send out invites.

Michael: Please do that.

David: Let’s aim for Tuesday morning.

Priya: Noted, I’ll send the invite for Tuesday.

Michael: Great, thanks everyone. Let’s move quickly on this.
